Indian Breakfast Recipes

Dosa

Dosa is a popular South Indian breakfast crepe made from lentils and rice. Coconut and/or ginger chutney as a side adds spice. Eating with lentil soup and fry made from mixed veg curry especially potato/carrot/peas/onion makes it more rich in taste as well as calories. It can be served not only for breakfast but for lunch or dinner as well.

Other popular breakfast items are idli, vada (deep fried spicy doughnut), moongdal crepe with upma, and puri with aloo curry. All of the above ones are a great choice for vegetarians. They provide an appropriate balance in carbohydrate/protein ratio.



Ingredients:

  • Urad dal - 1 cup.
  • Uncooked rice - 2 cups.
  • Fenugreek seeds - 1/2 tsp.

Steps for preparation:

  1. Soak urad dal, rice and fenugreek seeds together in water for 4 to 5 hours. Wash it thoroughly.
  2. Grind it into batter consistency adding little water.
  3. Let it ferment for 4 to 5 hrs, refrigerate and it is ready to use.
  4. Take a couple of scoops of batter into a separate bowl, add salt and water to make it to a pouring consistency.
  5. Heat the crepe pan on medium heat, spread the batter on to it. Drizzle some oil and let it cook until golden brown. Each one will take around 2 to 3 min to cook.
  6. Serve them hot with any of the sides mentioned above.
